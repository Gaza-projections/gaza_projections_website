Welcome to Our Project

Since 7 October 2023, armed conflict in the Gaza Strip, Palestine has led to a large number of traumatic deaths and injuries, extensive forced displacement, closure of health services, deteriorating living conditions, and worsening food security.

There is some information available on total deaths. While people are preoccupied with direct war-related fatalities in the first instance, less is known about excess mortality due to the indirect effects of the crisis due to disruption of health services, overcrowding, inadequate water, sanitation and hygiene and insufficient food intake.

Our group has developed models to project excess deaths, both directly and indirectly crisis-attributable, that may occur in Gaza over the six-month period from 7 February 2024 to 6 August 2024. This will be done in consideration of one of three scenarios: a ceasefire, a continuation of conditions experienced between 15 October to 15 January, termed “status quo,” and a further escalation of the war. Our projections are stratified by sub-period, age and category of proximal causes of death (infections, non-communicable diseases, maternal, and babies, and trauma injuries). Over the next few months, we expect to publish updates of our projections; the scenarios may be altered to reflect the evolution of the crisis, improved data, or new models.
